Oyinkan Ifidon-Ola is a researcher & designer whose work is positioned at the intersection of indigenous design and civic imagination.

Her dual degree in Information Science and Design from the University of Michigan trained her to see patterns in data, and meaning in form. She has centered her practice on community engagement and urban & architectural research.

Grounded in design as praxis and co-citizenship, her approach treats making as a form of critical engagement—where theory and action continuously inform one another. She is currently exploring how carcerality functions to restrict the agency, and spatial rights of marginalized populations.
